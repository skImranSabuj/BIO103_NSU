## 🔬 Lec 13: Cell Cycle and DNA Replication

---

**1.** Which phase of the cell cycle is responsible for DNA replication?

A) G1 phase
B) G2 phase
C) M phase
D) S phase

**Answer: D**
_S (Synthesis) phase is specifically dedicated to copying all DNA._

---

**2.** The term "Interphase" refers to which combination of phases?

A) M + C
B) G1 + S + G2
C) S + M + C
D) G2 + M

**Answer: B**
_Interphase = G1 + S + G2, which occupies ~90% of the total cycle._

---

**3.** Why must DNA be replicated before cell division?

A) To increase the cell's size
B) To produce more protein
C) So each daughter cell receives a complete and identical set of genetic instructions
D) To prepare for binary fission only

**Answer: C**
_DNA carries instructions for cell function — every new cell must get a full copy._

---

**4.** Approximately what percentage of the total cell cycle time does interphase occupy?

A) 10%
B) 50%
C) 75%
D) 90%

**Answer: D**
_Interphase is the longest phase; mitosis itself is relatively quick._

---

**5.** DNA is tightly coiled around proteins called:

A) Centromeres
B) Chromatids
C) Histones
D) Spindle fibers

**Answer: C**
_Histones are proteins that DNA wraps around to compact into chromosomes._

---

**6.** Human somatic cells contain how many chromosomes?

A) 23
B) 46
C) 48
D) 92

**Answer: B**
_Humans have 46 chromosomes arranged as 23 pairs in body (somatic) cells._

---

**7.** Two identical copies of a chromosome joined at one point are called:

A) Homologous chromosomes
B) Chromatids
C) Sister chromatids
D) Centrosomes

**Answer: C**
_After DNA replication, the two copies stay joined as sister chromatids until anaphase._

---

**8.** The point where sister chromatids are joined together is called:

A) Centrosome
B) Kinetochore
C) Nucleosome
D) Centromere

**Answer: D**
_The centromere is the constricted region holding sister chromatids together._

---

**9.** In prokaryotes, the chromosome is:

A) Multiple and linear
B) Single and circular
C) Paired and linear
D) Absent entirely

**Answer: B**
_Prokaryotes have one single circular chromosome attached to the cell membrane._

---

**10.** What is the main activity during G1 phase?

A) DNA synthesis
B) Nuclear division
C) Cell growth and preparation for DNA synthesis
D) Cytoplasmic division

**Answer: C**
_G1 (Gap 1) is the initial growth phase where the cell grows and prepares._

---

**11.** G2 phase is best described as:

A) DNA synthesis
B) Nuclear division
C) Further growth and preparation of structures needed for mitosis
D) Resting phase

**Answer: C**
_G2 (Gap 2) is a second growth phase where the cell prepares proteins for division._

---

**12.** Which of these is NOT a phase of the cell cycle?

A) G1
B) S
C) P
D) M

**Answer: C**
_There is no P phase in the cell cycle; P (Prophase) is a stage inside M phase._

---

**13.** The M phase of the cell cycle refers to:

A) S phase
B) Interphase
C) Mitosis (nuclear division)
D) G2 phase

**Answer: C**
_M phase is the mitotic phase where the nucleus divides._

---

**14.** What is the S in "S phase" of the cell cycle?

A) Separation
B) Spindle
C) Sister
D) Synthesis

**Answer: D**
_S phase = Synthesis phase, where DNA is synthesized (copied)._

---

**15.** The prokaryotic chromosome is attached to the:

A) Nuclear membrane
B) Ribosome
C) Cell membrane
D) Mitochondria

**Answer: C**
_In bacteria, the circular chromosome is attached to the inside of the cell membrane._

---

**16.** Most eukaryotic cells have between how many chromosomes in their body cells?

A) 1–5
B) 10–50
C) 100–200
D) 50–100

**Answer: B**
_Eukaryotic cells typically have 10–50 chromosomes, though this varies widely._

---

**17.** At the end of the S phase, a human cell has how many copies of its DNA?

A) 1 copy (unreplicated)
B) 2 identical copies
C) 4 copies
D) Half a copy

**Answer: B**
_DNA replication in S phase produces exactly two identical copies of the genome._

---

**18.** Cell division is necessary for all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Growth from embryo to adult
B) Replacing worn-out cells
C) Healing wounds
D) Converting glucose to energy

**Answer: D**
_Energy conversion happens through cellular respiration, not cell division._

---

**19.** Which best defines a chromosome?

A) A protein that aids cell division
B) An organelle in the cytoplasm
C) A structure made of DNA and protein that carries genetic information
D) A type of cell membrane

**Answer: C**
_A chromosome is a condensed structure of DNA wrapped around histone proteins._

---

**20.** After DNA replication, each chromosome consists of:

A) One chromatid
B) Four chromatids
C) Two sister chromatids joined at the centromere
D) Two separate chromosomes

**Answer: C**
_One original + one new copy = two sister chromatids joined at the centromere._

---

**21.** The cell cycle is considered complete after:

A) G2 phase
B) S phase
C) Nuclear division (M phase) only
D) Cytokinesis (C phase)

**Answer: D**
_The full cycle ends when the cytoplasm divides and two daughter cells are fully formed._

---

## 🔬 Lec 14: Mitosis, Meiosis, and Asexual Reproduction

---

**1.** Which is the correct sequence of mitosis stages?

A) Metaphase → Prophase → Anaphase → Telophase
B) Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase
C) Anaphase → Prophase → Metaphase → Telophase
D) Telophase → Anaphase → Metaphase → Prophase

**Answer: B**
_PMAT — Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase._

---

**2.** The stage where chromosomes align at the center of the cell is:

A) Prophase
B) Anaphase
C) Metaphase
D) Telophase

**Answer: C**
_In metaphase, chromosomes line up at the metaphase plate (cell's equator)._

---

**3.** During anaphase of mitosis, what happens?

A) Chromosomes condense
B) Nuclear membrane dissolves
C) Sister chromatids separate and move to opposite poles
D) Chromosomes line up in the middle

**Answer: C**
_Spindle fibers shorten, pulling sister chromatids to opposite poles._

---

**4.** Mitosis produces how many daughter cells with what chromosome number?

A) 4 haploid cells
B) 2 diploid cells identical to the parent
C) 2 haploid cells
D) 4 diploid cells

**Answer: B**
_Mitosis → 2 identical, diploid (2n) daughter cells._

---

**5.** Meiosis produces how many cells?

A) 2 diploid cells
B) 2 haploid cells
C) 4 haploid cells
D) 4 diploid cells

**Answer: C**
_Two rounds of division → 4 haploid (n) cells._

---

**6.** Which type of cells undergo meiosis?

A) Somatic (body) cells
B) Germ cells (in gonads)
C) Nerve cells
D) Bone cells

**Answer: B**
_Meiosis occurs in reproductive organs to produce gametes._

---

**7.** In humans, the haploid chromosome number (n) is:

A) 46
B) 92
C) 23
D) 48

**Answer: C**
_Gametes carry 23 chromosomes — half of the 46 in body cells._

---

**8.** Meiosis I is called "reduction division" because:

A) The cell shrinks in size
B) The chromosome number is reduced from diploid (2n) to haploid (n)
C) Only cytoplasm is divided
D) DNA is destroyed

**Answer: B**
_Meiosis I separates homologous chromosome pairs, halving the chromosome number._

---

**9.** Binary fission differs from mitosis mainly because:

A) Binary fission produces 4 cells
B) Binary fission occurs in cells that lack a true nucleus (prokaryotes)
C) Binary fission requires a spindle apparatus
D) Binary fission reduces chromosome number

**Answer: B**
_Prokaryotes have no nucleus, so their division cannot be mitosis._

---

**10.** Parthenogenesis is the development of an individual from:

A) A fertilized egg
B) Budding
C) An unfertilized egg
D) Spore germination

**Answer: C**
_In parthenogenesis, no fertilization occurs — the egg develops independently._

---

**11.** In budding:

A) The parent splits equally into two
B) An unfertilized egg develops
C) A small outgrowth develops on the parent and later detaches
D) Spores are released

**Answer: C**
_Example: Hydra reproduces by growing buds that pinch off._

---

**12.** Vegetative reproduction is mainly used by:

A) Animals
B) Bacteria
C) Plants (runners, tubers, bulbs)
D) Viruses

**Answer: C**
_Strawberry runners, potato tubers, and onion bulbs all use vegetative reproduction._

---

**13.** The main purpose of meiosis in sexually reproducing organisms is:

A) Repair damaged tissues
B) Produce haploid gametes for sexual reproduction
C) Replace somatic cells
D) Increase body size

**Answer: B**
_Gametes formed by meiosis fuse during fertilization to restore the diploid number._

---

**14.** During telophase of mitosis:

A) Chromosomes condense
B) Sister chromatids separate
C) Nuclear membranes reform around each chromosome set
D) Chromosomes line up at the plate

**Answer: C**
_Telophase is the reverse of prophase — the nucleus reforms._

---

**15.** During prophase of mitosis:

A) Chromosomes reach opposite poles
B) Chromosomes condense, nuclear membrane dissolves, and spindle begins to form
C) Chromosomes line up at the equator
D) Cytoplasm divides

**Answer: B**
_Prophase is the preparation stage: visible chromosomes, spindle formation._

---

**16.** Spermatogenesis (sperm formation) is meiosis occurring in:

A) Ovaries
B) Liver
C) Kidney
D) Testes

**Answer: D**
_Sperm are produced by meiosis in the testes; eggs in the ovaries._

---

**17.** Cytokinesis refers to the division of:

A) The nucleus
B) DNA
C) The cytoplasm into two separate cells
D) Chromosomes at the centromere

**Answer: C**
_Cytokinesis physically separates the cytoplasm to form two daughter cells._

---

**18.** Regeneration as asexual reproduction is seen in:

A) Only bacteria
B) Only mammals
C) Organisms like Hydra, starfish, and many plants
D) Only viruses

**Answer: C**
_Starfish can regenerate an entire organism from a single arm._

---

**19.** After meiosis II, each resulting cell contains:

A) The same chromosome number as the original (2n)
B) Twice the original number (4n)
C) Half the original chromosome number (n) — haploid
D) No chromosomes

**Answer: C**
_Both rounds of meiosis together halve chromosome number to n._

---

**20.** Which organism typically reproduces by spore formation?

A) Dogs
B) Starfish
C) Bacteria, fungi, and ferns
D) Whales

**Answer: C**
_Spore formation is common in prokaryotes, fungi, and lower plants._

---

**21.** The key difference between mitosis and meiosis is:

A) Mitosis is faster
B) Mitosis maintains chromosome number; meiosis halves it
C) Meiosis occurs in all body cells
D) Mitosis occurs only in gonads

**Answer: B**
_Mitosis → 2n daughter cells; Meiosis → n daughter cells._

---

**22.** A diploid cell (2n = 46) undergoes meiosis. Each resulting gamete has:

A) 46 chromosomes
B) 92 chromosomes
C) 23 chromosomes
D) 12 chromosomes

**Answer: C**
_Meiosis halves the chromosome number: 46 ÷ 2 = 23._

---

## 🧬 Lec 15: Homeostasis

---

**1.** Homeostasis is best defined as:

A) The production of hormones by endocrine glands
B) Maintenance of a constant internal environment despite external changes
C) The digestion of food into absorbable molecules
D) The immune response to infection

**Answer: B**
_Homeostasis literally means "same state" — keeping the internal environment stable._

---

**2.** The fluid that directly surrounds body cells and forms the internal environment is:

A) Blood plasma
B) Lymph
C) Tissue fluid
D) Urine

**Answer: C**
_Tissue fluid (interstitial fluid) bathes cells and is the true internal environment._

---

**3.** The body's main thermoregulatory centre is located in:

A) The pituitary gland
B) The hypothalamus
C) The adrenal gland
D) The kidney

**Answer: B**
_The anterior hypothalamus contains the thermo-regulatory centre (TRC)._

---

**4.** The normal core body temperature maintained by homeostasis is:

A) 35°C
B) 40°C ± 1°C
C) 37°C ± 1°C
D) 32°C ± 1°C

**Answer: C**
_37°C is the optimal temperature for enzyme activity and metabolism._

---

**5.** The three components of a feedback mechanism in homeostasis are:

A) Heart, lungs, kidneys
B) Sense organ (receptor), control centre, responding organ (effector)
C) Hormones, nerves, muscles only
D) Glucose, insulin, glucagon

**Answer: B**
_Receptor detects change → control centre processes → effector responds._

---

**6.** Negative feedback in homeostasis means:

A) The response amplifies the original change
B) No response occurs to the change
C) The response opposes and reverses the original change to restore balance
D) Body temperature always rises

**Answer: C**
_Negative feedback is the most common homeostatic mechanism — it corrects deviations._

---

**7.** Vasodilation during hot weather helps cool the body by:

A) Reducing blood flow to the skin
B) Increasing heat retention
C) Bringing more blood near the skin surface so more heat is lost to surroundings
D) Causing shivering

**Answer: C**
_More blood near skin = more radiation/convection of heat away from the body._

---

**8.** During cold weather, the body conserves heat by:

A) Vasodilation and sweating
B) Vasoconstriction (less blood near skin surface) and shivering
C) Increasing urine output
D) Dilating skin blood vessels

**Answer: B**
_Vasoconstriction + shivering (heat generation by muscle contractions) conserves heat._

---

**9.** When blood becomes too dilute after drinking large amounts of water, the kidneys:

A) Produce more concentrated urine
B) Produce more dilute urine to excrete excess water
C) Stop producing urine completely
D) Increase salt reabsorption only

**Answer: B**
_Kidneys excrete more dilute urine to remove the excess water._

---

**10.** Shivering generates heat because:

A) It cools the body by evaporation
B) Rapid muscle contractions release heat as a by-product
C) It dilates blood vessels
D) It increases sweating

**Answer: B**
_Muscle contractions use ATP; the energy released as heat warms the body._

---

**11.** The hormone that lowers blood glucose as part of homeostasis is:

A) Glucagon
B) Adrenaline
C) Insulin
D) Thyroxine

**Answer: C**
_Insulin signals cells to take up glucose and the liver to store it as glycogen._

---

**12.** Which is NOT a homeostatic function of the kidneys?

A) Regulating water balance
B) Excreting urea
C) Producing insulin
D) Controlling blood pH

**Answer: C**
_Insulin is produced by the pancreas, not the kidneys._

---

**13.** The endocrine system contributes to homeostasis mainly through:

A) Nerve impulses
B) Hormones carried in the bloodstream to target organs
C) Direct cell-to-cell contact
D) Mechanical movement

**Answer: B**
_Endocrine glands secrete hormones into blood for transport to distant target cells._

---

**14.** Compared to the endocrine system, the nervous system provides homeostatic control that is:

A) Slower and longer lasting
B) Absent from temperature regulation
C) Rapid and short-duration (milliseconds)
D) Only hormonal

**Answer: C**
_Nerve impulses travel very fast; endocrine responses are slower but more sustained._

---

**15.** Osmoregulation by the kidneys involves controlling:

A) Oxygen levels
B) Core body temperature
C) Water and ion (salt) balance in body fluids
D) Carbohydrate digestion

**Answer: C**
_Osmoregulation prevents cells from swelling or shrinking due to osmotic imbalance._

---

**16.** Urea, excreted by the kidneys, is produced from:

A) Carbohydrate breakdown
B) Fat digestion in the small intestine
C) Protein (amino acid) metabolism in the liver
D) Glucose oxidation in mitochondria

**Answer: C**
_When amino acids are broken down, the amino group is converted to urea in the liver._

---

**17.** The fat layer beneath the skin (hypodermis) functions in homeostasis by:

A) Producing sweat
B) Insulating the body to retain heat in cold conditions
C) Dilating blood vessels
D) Secreting hormones

**Answer: B**
_Subcutaneous fat is a natural insulator that reduces heat loss._

---

**18.** In the feedback loop, the "effector" is:

A) The receptor that detects the change
B) The brain processing centre
C) The organ that carries out the corrective response
D) The blood vessel

**Answer: C**
_Effectors (muscles, glands, kidneys) execute the correction after receiving instructions._

---

**19.** Blood pH in a healthy person is maintained within:

A) 6.0–6.4
B) 7.35–7.45
C) 8.0–8.5
D) 5.5–6.0

**Answer: B**
_The blood must stay slightly alkaline; deviations cause serious illness._

---

**20.** The thyroid gland contributes to homeostasis by regulating:

A) Blood glucose
B) Water balance
C) Metabolic rate (through thyroxine)
D) Blood clotting

**Answer: C**
_Thyroxine increases metabolic rate — too little causes fatigue; too much causes anxiety._

---

**21.** Which statement about homeostasis is correct?

A) It only involves temperature control
B) It is a one-time correction, not ongoing
C) It maintains conditions close to a set point using continuous feedback loops
D) Only the kidneys are involved

**Answer: C**
_Homeostasis is a dynamic, continuous process using multiple organ systems._

---

## 🫁 Lec 19: Respiratory System

---

**1.** The primary site of gas exchange in the lungs is:

A) Trachea
B) Bronchi
C) Alveoli
D) Bronchioles

**Answer: C**
_Alveoli are tiny air sacs with thin walls and dense capillary networks — perfect for diffusion._

---

**2.** Gas exchange in the alveoli occurs by:

A) Active transport (requires energy)
B) Osmosis
C) Simple diffusion (high concentration → low concentration)
D) Facilitated diffusion through carrier proteins

**Answer: C**
_No energy is needed — gases move passively down their concentration gradients._

---

**3.** Oxygen moves from the alveoli into the blood because:

A) It is actively pumped by the heart
B) Its concentration in inhaled alveolar air is higher than in deoxygenated blood
C) Its concentration is lower in the alveolus than in the blood
D) Hemoglobin pulls it through the alveolar wall

**Answer: B**
_High O₂ in alveolar air → diffuses into blood with low O₂._

---

**4.** Carbon dioxide moves from blood into the alveoli because:

A) Its concentration is higher in alveolar air than in blood
B) Its concentration is higher in blood (from cellular respiration) than in the alveolus
C) It is actively transported out
D) It is attracted by oxygen in the alveolus

**Answer: B**
_Blood returning from tissues is loaded with CO₂ → diffuses into fresh alveolar air._

---

**5.** The correct order of the air pathway from outside to the alveoli is:

A) Nose → Trachea → Pharynx → Bronchi → Alveoli
B) Nose → Pharynx → Larynx → Trachea → Bronchi → Bronchioles → Alveoli
C) Nose → Larynx → Pharynx → Trachea → Alveoli
D) Mouth → Bronchi → Trachea → Pharynx → Alveoli

**Answer: B**
_Memory trick: Never Play Loud Tunes Before Breakfast Always._

---

**6.** Cellular respiration is best described as:

A) The physical act of breathing in and out
B) A biochemical process inside cells that releases energy (ATP) from food molecules
C) The pumping of blood to the lungs
D) Removal of CO₂ from alveoli only

**Answer: B**
_Breathing = physical; cellular respiration = chemical (occurs in mitochondria)._

---

**7.** The trachea (windpipe) is kept permanently open by:

A) Elastic fibers
B) Muscle contractions
C) Mucus only
D) C-shaped cartilage rings

**Answer: D**
_Incomplete cartilage rings give structural support while allowing the esophagus behind it to expand._

---

**8.** The alveolar wall is specially adapted for gas exchange because it is:

A) Multi-layered and thick for protection
B) Made of impermeable cartilage
C) Only one cell thick, minimizing diffusion distance
D) Filled with mucus

**Answer: C**
_One-cell-thick walls + large surface area + moist lining = maximum diffusion rate._

---

**9.** The products of aerobic cellular respiration are:

A) Glucose and oxygen
B) Carbon dioxide, water, and ATP
C) Nitrogen and water only
D) Only ATP

**Answer: B**
_Glucose + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + ATP_

---

**10.** After gas exchange in the lungs, oxygenated blood returns to the heart via the:

A) Aorta
B) Pulmonary artery
C) Pulmonary vein
D) Vena cava

**Answer: C**
_Pulmonary vein = oxygenated blood from lungs → left atrium._

---

**11.** Inhaled air contains approximately what percentage of oxygen?

A) 0.034%
B) 4%
C) 79%
D) 21%

**Answer: D**
_Air is ~21% O₂; exhaled air is ~16% O₂ (some was absorbed)._

---

**12.** The larynx is important for:

A) Gas exchange
B) Housing the vocal cords and passing air to the trachea
C) Filtering dust particles
D) Bronchiole branching

**Answer: B**
_The larynx is the voice box and sits between the pharynx and trachea._

---

**13.** The respiratory system works most directly with which other system for oxygen delivery to all tissues?

A) Digestive system
B) Skeletal system
C) Circulatory (cardiovascular) system
D) Reproductive system

**Answer: C**
_Blood picks up O₂ from the lungs and delivers it to every cell in the body._

---

**14.** The nasal cavity filters inhaled air using:

A) Alveoli
B) Hairs and mucus that trap particles and pathogens
C) Cartilage rings
D) Vocal cords

**Answer: B**
_The nose also warms and moistens air before it reaches the lungs._

---

**15.** Bronchi branch into progressively smaller airways called:

A) Alveoli
B) Capillaries
C) Bronchioles
D) Lacteals

**Answer: C**
_Bronchi → bronchioles → alveolar ducts → alveoli._

---

**16.** What is exhalation?

A) Drawing air into the lungs
B) Gas exchange at alveoli
C) The process of pushing air (rich in CO₂) out of the lungs
D) O₂ binding to hemoglobin

**Answer: C**
_Exhalation removes CO₂-laden air; inhalation brings in fresh O₂-rich air._

---

**17.** Why do alveoli have such a large combined surface area?

A) To store more air in the lungs
B) Because there are hundreds of millions of them in each lung
C) To slow down gas exchange
D) To produce mucus

**Answer: B**
_~700 million alveoli per lung create an enormous surface area for rapid diffusion._

---

**18.** The diaphragm contracts during:

A) Exhalation only
B) Gas exchange in alveoli
C) Inhalation — it flattens and increases chest volume
D) Swallowing

**Answer: C**
_Diaphragm contraction + rib elevation = increased lung volume = air flows in._

---

**19.** The pharynx is best described as:

A) The windpipe
B) The voice box
C) A shared passage for both air and food connecting mouth/nose to larynx/esophagus
D) The main site of gas exchange

**Answer: C**
_The pharynx is a shared airway and food passage — this is why we can choke._

---

**20.** Emphysema damages the lungs by:

A) Narrowing the trachea
B) Destroying alveolar walls, reducing gas exchange surface area
C) Blocking the bronchi with mucus only
D) Strengthening alveoli

**Answer: B**
_Fewer alveoli = less surface area = less O₂ absorbed = breathlessness._

---

**21.** Which statement correctly contrasts breathing and cellular respiration?

A) Both are purely physical processes
B) Breathing is chemical; cellular respiration is physical
C) Breathing is a mechanical process; cellular respiration is a biochemical process in cells
D) They are identical processes

**Answer: C**
_Breathing moves air; cellular respiration uses O₂ to extract energy from food._

---

## 🩺 Lec 20: Diabetes and Lipid Profile

---

**1.** Diabetes mellitus is caused by problems with:

A) Hemoglobin production
B) Insulin secretion or action
C) Adrenaline overproduction
D) Thyroxine deficiency

**Answer: B**
_Diabetes is a disorder of glucose metabolism caused by insulin deficiency or resistance._

---

**2.** Hyperglycemia means:

A) Low blood sugar
B) High blood pressure
C) Abnormally high blood glucose levels
D) Low blood pressure

**Answer: C**
_Hyperglycemia = hyper (high) + glycemia (blood glucose)._

---

**3.** Insulin is produced by which cells of the pancreas?

A) Alpha cells
B) Delta cells
C) Beta (islet) cells
D) Liver cells

**Answer: C**
_Beta islet cells in the pancreas synthesize and release insulin._

---

**4.** In normal glucose metabolism, after a meal blood glucose rises and then:

A) Glucagon is released to raise blood sugar further
B) Insulin is released → cells absorb glucose → blood sugar returns to normal
C) Adrenaline removes glucose from blood
D) The liver produces more glucose

**Answer: B**
_Insulin is the key signal for glucose uptake and storage._

---

**5.** In Type 1 diabetes, insulin secretion is:

A) Elevated above normal
B) Normal
C) Very low or absent due to autoimmune destruction of beta cells
D) Slightly reduced

**Answer: C**
_The immune system attacks beta cells, destroying the body's ability to make insulin._

---

**6.** Type 2 diabetes is most commonly associated with:

A) Autoimmune disease in children
B) Insulin resistance in adults, often linked to obesity and inactivity
C) Pregnancy only
D) Complete absence of beta cells

**Answer: B**
_~95% of all diabetes cases are Type 2; it is strongly linked to lifestyle factors._

---

**7.** In diabetes, glucose accumulates in the blood because:

A) The kidneys excrete too little glucose
B) Insulin is absent, insufficient, or ineffective — cells cannot absorb glucose properly
C) The liver produces excess glycogen
D) Blood vessels are blocked

**Answer: B**
_Without effective insulin signaling, glucose cannot enter muscle and fat cells._

---

**8.** Gestational diabetes occurs:

A) Only in children under 5
B) Only as a progression of Type 2
C) For the first time during pregnancy
D) Only in elderly patients

**Answer: C**
_It usually resolves after delivery but increases future Type 2 risk._

---

**9.** A baby born to a mother with untreated gestational diabetes may experience:

A) Low birth weight only
B) Macrosomia (abnormally large size), hypoglycemia after birth, and breathing difficulties
C) Premature aging
D) No complications

**Answer: B**
_Excess maternal glucose crosses the placenta, causing the baby to grow too large._

---

**10.** LDL cholesterol is called "bad" cholesterol because:

A) It carries cholesterol away from arteries
B) Excess LDL deposits inside artery walls, causing atherosclerosis
C) It raises HDL levels
D) It is stored in the liver safely

**Answer: B**
_High LDL → plaque buildup → narrowed arteries → heart attack / stroke risk._

---

**11.** HDL cholesterol is "good" because:

A) It builds plaques in arteries
B) It raises blood pressure
C) It carries excess cholesterol back to the liver for removal
D) It reduces insulin production

**Answer: C**
_Higher HDL = more cholesterol removed from circulation = lower cardiovascular risk._

---

**12.** An optimal LDL level is:

A) Above 190 mg/dL
B) 130–160 mg/dL
C) Below 100 mg/dL
D) Below 40 mg/dL

**Answer: C**
_LDL < 100 is optimal; ≥ 190 is very high risk._

---

**13.** The liver stores excess blood glucose as:

A) Starch
B) Cellulose
C) Glycogen
D) Fructose

**Answer: C**
_Glycogen is the animal storage form of glucose — insulin triggers this conversion._

---

**14.** A lipid profile test includes all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Total cholesterol
B) LDL and HDL
C) Triglycerides
D) Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)

**Answer: D**
_HbA1c is a diabetes monitoring test, not a standard lipid profile component._

---

**15.** A total cholesterol level of 250 mg/dL is classified as:

A) Optimal (< 200)
B) Borderline (200–239)
C) High risk (≥ 240)
D) Normal

**Answer: C**
_Total cholesterol ≥ 240 mg/dL = high risk for cardiovascular disease._

---

**16.** Triglycerides above 200 mg/dL are classified as:

A) Optimal
B) Normal
C) Borderline
D) High

**Answer: D**
_< 150 = normal; 150–199 = borderline; ≥ 200 = high._

---

**17.** Treatment that is absolutely essential for Type 1 diabetics is:

A) Diet changes alone
B) Exercise alone
C) Oral tablets only
D) Insulin injections (since no insulin is produced)

**Answer: D**
_Without insulin replacement, Type 1 diabetics cannot survive — glucose cannot enter cells._

---

**18.** Which patient profile has the highest cardiovascular risk based on lipid values?

A) Low LDL + high HDL
B) High LDL + low HDL + high triglycerides
C) Normal total cholesterol + high HDL
D) Low triglycerides + high HDL

**Answer: B**
_Each component in option B independently increases risk; combined they are very dangerous._

---

**19.** An optimal protective HDL level is:

A) Less than 40 mg/dL
B) 40–59 mg/dL
C) 60 mg/dL or higher
D) Above 200 mg/dL

**Answer: C**
_HDL ≥ 60 is considered protective against heart disease._

---

**20.** Gestational diabetes typically:

A) Persists for life in all cases
B) Resolves after delivery but raises the mother's future risk of Type 2 diabetes
C) Always converts to Type 1 diabetes after delivery
D) Has no lasting effect on the mother at all

**Answer: B**
_~50% of women with gestational diabetes develop Type 2 within 5–10 years._

---

**21.** Atherosclerosis is the progressive buildup of:

A) Glycogen in arteries
B) Fatty plaques (mainly cholesterol) inside artery walls, narrowing the vessel
C) Muscle tissue in veins
D) White blood cells in capillaries

**Answer: B**
_Plaque → narrowed lumen → reduced blood flow → heart attack or stroke._

## 🥗 Lec 21: Nutrients and Energy

---

**1.** Which nutrient class is the body's primary and preferred source of energy?

A) Protein
B) Fat
C) Carbohydrate
D) Vitamins

**Answer: C**
_Carbohydrates are digested to glucose — the immediate fuel for cells and the brain._

---

**2.** How many calories (Cal/kcal) does 1 gram of fat provide?

A) 4
B) 7
C) 9
D) 2

**Answer: C**
_Fat = 9 Cal/g; more than twice the energy density of carbohydrate or protein._

---

**3.** How many calories does 1 gram of carbohydrate OR protein each provide?

A) 9
B) 7
C) 4
D) 2

**Answer: C**
_Both carbohydrate and protein provide 4 Cal/g._

---

**4.** Which nutrient class does NOT directly provide energy (calories)?

A) Carbohydrates
B) Fats
C) Proteins
D) Vitamins

**Answer: D**
_Vitamins (and minerals and water) do not yield calories when metabolized._

---

**5.** The six classes of nutrients are:

A) Glucose, fat, amino acids, iron, calcium, water
B) Starch, sugar, oil, salt, fiber, water
C) Carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins, minerals, water
D) Enzymes, hormones, fat, protein, minerals, glucose

**Answer: C**
_These six classes encompass all essential dietary components._

---

**6.** Protein is described as "not the preferred energy source" because:

A) It cannot be digested
B) It provides less energy than water
C) Its primary role is structural — building and repairing tissues, enzymes, hormones
D) It contains no carbon

**Answer: C**
_The body burns carbohydrate and fat first; protein is conserved for structural use._

---

**7.** Starch from food is ultimately broken down in the body to:

A) Glycogen
B) Glucose (the primary cellular fuel)
C) Amino acids
D) Fatty acids

**Answer: B**
_Amylases in saliva and the pancreas convert starch → glucose._

---

**8.** Which best describes the role of fats in the body?

A) Primary fuel for the brain only
B) Concentrated energy store, cell membrane component, carrier of fat-soluble vitamins
C) Building block for hemoglobin only
D) Only used for insulation

**Answer: B**
_Fats serve multiple roles beyond energy: structural and transport functions._

---

**9.** A nutrient is formally defined as:

A) Any food you eat
B) A raw material that provides essential matter and energy for life
C) A vitamin supplement only
D) Only energy-producing compounds

**Answer: B**
_Not all food components are nutrients; some are fillers or additives._

---

**10.** All three energy-providing nutrient classes (carbohydrate, fat, protein) share which element?

A) Nitrogen
B) Phosphorus
C) Carbon (they are all carbon-based compounds)
D) Sulfur

**Answer: C**
_All energy macronutrients are organic — carbon-containing — compounds._

---

**11.** Excess dietary energy is stored in the body mainly as:

A) Only as proteins in muscle
B) Glycogen in liver and muscle, and fat in adipose tissue
C) Only as minerals
D) Water in blood

**Answer: B**
_Short-term: glycogen; long-term energy reserve: triglycerides in adipose tissue._

---

**12.** Dietary fiber is a type of carbohydrate that is important because it:

A) Provides the highest caloric energy
B) Aids digestion, bowel regularity, and may reduce cholesterol absorption
C) Replaces vitamins in the diet
D) Is stored as glycogen

**Answer: B**
_Fiber is not digested for energy but is essential for gut health._

---

**13.** Which food group provides the most complex carbohydrates?

A) Meat and dairy
B) Butter and oils
C) Whole grains, legumes (peas, beans), and vegetables
D) Sugar-sweetened beverages

**Answer: C**
_Complex carbs (starch + fiber) from whole foods = healthier than simple sugars._

---

**14.** The nutrient with the highest energy density per gram is:

A) Carbohydrate (4 Cal/g)
B) Protein (4 Cal/g)
C) Water (0 Cal/g)
D) Fat (9 Cal/g)

**Answer: D**
_Fat provides more than double the calories per gram compared to carb or protein._

---

**15.** The body's long-term energy reserve is stored primarily as:

A) Blood glucose
B) Glycogen in liver
C) Fat in adipose (body fat) tissue
D) Protein in muscle

**Answer: C**
_Fat stores are nearly unlimited compared to glycogen stores._

---

**16.** Protein molecules are built from subunits called:

A) Fatty acids
B) Monosaccharides
C) Amino acids
D) Nucleotides

**Answer: C**
_There are 20 amino acids; proteins are chains of amino acids in specific sequences._

---

**17.** An essential amino acid is one that:

A) The body can synthesize in large amounts
B) Must be obtained from food because the body cannot synthesize it
C) Provides the most energy
D) Is only found in plants

**Answer: B**
_9 of the 20 amino acids are "essential" — they must come from diet._

---

**18.** Unsaturated fats compared to saturated fats are generally:

A) More harmful for heart health
B) Healthier — associated with lower cardiovascular risk
C) Not sources of energy
D) Identical in health effects

**Answer: B**
_Unsaturated fats (olive oil, fish) are associated with better cardiovascular outcomes._

---

**19.** The daily caloric recommendation for carbohydrates in a typical balanced diet is approximately:

A) 10–15% of total calories
B) 45–65% of total calories
C) 80–90% of total calories
D) Less than 5%

**Answer: B**
_Carbohydrates should provide ~50–65% of daily calories in a balanced diet._

---

**20.** Which statement is correct about water as a nutrient?

A) Water provides 4 Cal/g
B) Water is only needed for digestion
C) Water provides no calories but is essential as a medium for all biochemical reactions, transport, temperature regulation, and excretion

**Answer: C**
_The body is ~60% water — every metabolic process requires it._

---

**21.** Which macronutrient builds enzymes and hormones in the body?

A) Carbohydrate
B) Fat
C) Protein

**Answer: C**
_Enzymes and most hormones (e.g., insulin) are proteins._

## 🥦 Lec 22: Vitamins, Minerals, BMI, and Malnutrition

---

**1.** Which vitamins are fat-soluble?

A) A, B, C, D
B) B, C, D, E
C) A, D, E, K
D) C, D, E, K

**Answer: C**
_"ADEK" — A, D, E, K are fat-soluble; all others (B group, C) are water-soluble._

---

**2.** Fat-soluble vitamins are stored in:

A) Blood plasma
B) Muscle tissue only
C) Body fat (adipose tissue) and the liver
D) Bones only

**Answer: C**
_Because they are stored, fat-soluble vitamins can accumulate to toxic levels if over-consumed._

---

**3.** Why must water-soluble vitamins like vitamin C be consumed regularly?

A) They are toxic if stored
B) They are not stored in significant amounts and excess is excreted in urine
C) They are only made by gut bacteria
D) They dissolve only in fat

**Answer: B**
_Water-soluble vitamins pass quickly through the body — daily intake is needed._

---

**4.** The BMI formula is:

A) Weight (kg) × Height (m)
B) Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)
C) Height (cm) ÷ Weight (kg)
D) Weight (lb) ÷ Height (in)

**Answer: B**
_BMI = kg/m² — a simple ratio of weight to height squared._

---

**5.** A person weighing 70 kg with height 1.75 m has a BMI of:

A) 40
B) 18
C) 22.9 (normal range)
D) 30

**Answer: C**
_BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 70 ÷ 3.0625 ≈ 22.9 → Normal._

---

**6.** A BMI of 27 is classified as:

A) Underweight (< 18.5)
B) Normal (18.5–24.9)
C) Overweight (25–29.9)
D) Obese (≥ 30)

**Answer: C**
_25.0–29.9 = Overweight; ≥ 30 = Obese._

---

**7.** Iron deficiency most directly causes:

A) Rickets
B) Scurvy
C) Anemia (reduced hemoglobin / reduced oxygen-carrying capacity)
D) Goiter

**Answer: C**
_Iron is needed to make hemoglobin in red blood cells._

---

**8.** Iodine deficiency leads to:

A) Night blindness
B) Goiter (enlarged thyroid gland, unable to produce adequate thyroid hormone)
C) Scurvy
D) Rickets

**Answer: B**
_The thyroid needs iodine to make thyroxine; without it, the gland swells._

---

**9.** Vitamin A deficiency is associated with:

A) Scurvy
B) Goiter
C) Night blindness progressing to permanent blindness
D) Rickets

**Answer: C**
_Vitamin A forms rhodopsin in rod cells of the retina — essential for night vision._

---

**10.** Vitamin D is important for:

A) Blood clotting
B) Vision in low light
C) Promoting calcium absorption and maintaining bone health
D) Antioxidant protection of cells

**Answer: C**
_Without vitamin D, calcium cannot be properly absorbed from food._

---

**11.** Calcium in the diet is most important for:

A) Hemoglobin production
B) Fluid balance regulation
C) Building and maintaining bones and teeth (plus muscle contraction, nerve function)
D) Hormone production only

**Answer: C**
_99% of body calcium is in bones and teeth._

---

**12.** Water makes up approximately what percentage of total body weight?

A) 10–20%
B) 60–70%
C) 80–90%
D) 30–40%

**Answer: B**
_The body is about 60% water — more in lean individuals, less in those with more fat._

---

**13.** Overnutrition is best described as:

A) Deficiency of specific vitamins
B) Excess caloric intake leading to obesity and related chronic diseases
C) Too little food intake
D) Lack of minerals only

**Answer: B**
_Overnutrition is common in developed countries; it increases risk of heart disease, diabetes, and cancer._

---

**14.** A BMI below 18.5 indicates:

A) Obese
B) Overweight
C) Underweight
D) Normal

**Answer: C**
_BMI < 18.5 = underweight; associated with malnutrition, immune weakness, and infertility._

---

**15.** Vitamin K is important for:

A) Night vision
B) Blood clotting and calcium metabolism in bone
C) Immune function only
D) Cellular energy metabolism

**Answer: B**
_Vitamin K deficiency → impaired blood clotting → excessive bleeding._

---

**16.** Scurvy is caused by deficiency of:

A) Vitamin D
B) Vitamin A
C) Vitamin B12
D) Vitamin C

**Answer: D**
_Vitamin C is needed for collagen synthesis — deficiency causes bleeding gums and poor wound healing._

---

**17.** Rickets in children is caused by deficiency of:

A) Excess vitamin A
B) Vitamin D (and/or calcium)
C) Iron
D) Iodine

**Answer: B**
_Vitamin D deficiency → poor calcium absorption → soft, weak bones → rickets._

---

**18.** Sodium in the diet primarily contributes to:

A) Bone formation
B) Hemoglobin production
C) Fluid balance and nerve/muscle function
D) Hormone synthesis

**Answer: C**
_Sodium is the main extracellular cation — it regulates blood volume and nerve transmission._

---

**19.** A key limitation of BMI is that it:

A) Is too complex to calculate
B) Does not account for muscle mass — a muscular athlete may be "overweight" by BMI but have low body fat
C) Directly measures fat percentage
D) Is unaffected by age and sex

**Answer: B**
_BMI cannot distinguish between fat mass and lean muscle mass._

---

**20.** Malnutrition includes all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Not eating enough food (undernutrition)
B) Eating too much food (overnutrition)
C) Having an imbalanced diet that lacks a specific nutrient
D) Eating a complete, balanced diet that meets all nutritional needs

**Answer: D**
_A truly balanced diet by definition prevents malnutrition._

---

**21.** Vitamin E functions mainly as:

A) A blood clotting factor
B) A hormone precursor
C) An antioxidant that protects cell membranes from oxidative damage
D) A digestive enzyme

**Answer: C**
_Vitamin E neutralizes free radicals, protecting cells from oxidative stress._

---

**22.** Plants obtain their nutrients primarily through:

A) Eating other organisms
B) Photosynthesis (CO₂ + water + sunlight → glucose) and mineral absorption from soil
C) Absorbing proteins directly from soil
D) Bacterial symbiosis only

**Answer: B**
_Plants are autotrophs — they make their own organic nutrients using light energy._
