## Q1: Explain the cell theory and discuss its importance in biology.

### ✅ Discovery of Cells

- In the middle of the **17th century**, **Robert Hooke** (1635–1703) examined a piece of **cork tissue** with his microscope
- He saw numerous **box-shaped structures** that resembled rows of empty rooms
- He called them **"cells"**

---

### ✅ The Cell Theory

The Cell Theory has **three main principles:**

| #   | Principle                                                              | Simple Explanation                  |
| --- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Every organism is composed of one or more cells**                    | All living things are made of cells |
| 2   | **The cell is the smallest unit having properties of life**            | Cell = basic unit of life           |
| 3   | **Continuity of life arises from growth and division of single cells** | New cells come from existing cells  |

---

### ✅ Importance of Cell Theory in Biology

| Importance                | Explanation                                                |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Foundation of biology** | Cell theory is the central idea in all studies of biology  |
| **Understanding life**    | Tells us that all life starts from cells                   |
| **Medical science**       | Helps understand diseases at cellular level                |
| **Research basis**        | All biological research is built on understanding cells    |
| **Unity of life**         | Shows all organisms share the same basic structure (cells) |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Cell theory says: Everything alive is made of cells, cells are the smallest unit of life, and all cells come from other cells. It's the most fundamental idea in biology!

### 📝 Exam Style Answer

Cell theory states:

- All organisms are made of one or more cells
- Cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life
- New cells arise from pre-existing cells

Importance: It provides the foundation for understanding life, growth, disease, and modern biological research.

Summary: Cell theory is the core principle of biology.

## Q2: Differentiate between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.

### ✅ Two Types of Cells

Biologists have categorized cells into two general types:

---

### 🔑 Comparison Table

| Feature        | Prokaryotic Cell                          | Eukaryotic Cell                             |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **Cell type**  | Usually **unicellular**                   | Usually **multicellular**                   |
| **Nucleus**    | **No true nucleus** (nucleoid region)     | **Membrane-bound nucleus** ✅               |
| **Organelles** | **Absent** (no membrane-bound organelles) | **Present** (mitochondria, ER, Golgi, etc.) |
| **Ribosomes**  | **Smaller** ribosomes                     | **Larger** ribosomes                        |
| **DNA**        | Circular DNA in nucleoid                  | Linear DNA in chromosomes                   |
| **Examples**   | **Bacteria, Archaea**                     | **Animals, Plants, Fungi, Algae**           |

---

---

### ✅ Structure of the Plasma Membrane

> The **plasma membrane** is the boundary between the cell and its environment.

| Feature          | Detail                                                                                   |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Function**     | Isolates the cell, regulates what enters and leaves, allows interaction with other cells |
| **Composition**  | Made of **fats (phospholipids) and proteins**                                            |
| **Structure**    | **Lipid bilayer** — double fat layer with water-loving heads and water-hating tails      |
| **Permeability** | **Selectively permeable** — controls what goes in and out of the cell                    |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Prokaryotic = simple cell, no nucleus (bacteria). Eukaryotic = complex cell, has nucleus and organelles (plants, animals, fungi).

### 📝 Exam Style Answer

Prokaryotic cells are simple, usually unicellular, and lack a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles.
Eukaryotic cells are complex, have a membrane-bound nucleus and organelles, and include plant, animal, fungal, and protist cells.

Summary: Main difference is cellular complexity and presence of a true nucleus.

## Q3: Describe the structure and functions of the plasma membrane.

### ✅ Structure of the Plasma Membrane

The plasma membrane is made of a **lipid bilayer**:

| Component         | Description                                                                                                            |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Phospholipids** | Main structural component — each has a **hydrophilic head** (loves water) and two **hydrophobic tails** (repels water) |
| **Lipid bilayer** | Two layers of phospholipids arranged tail-to-tail                                                                      |
| **Proteins**      | Embedded in or attached to the membrane — help with transport and signaling                                            |
| **Fluidity**      | The lipid bilayer gives the membrane its fluid, flexible nature                                                        |

---

### ✅ Functions of the Plasma Membrane

| Function                      | Explanation                                              |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1. **Boundary**               | Acts as the barrier between the cell and its environment |
| 2. **Selective permeability** | Controls what molecules **enter and leave** the cell     |
| 3. **Transport**              | Allows exchange of materials (nutrients in, waste out)   |
| 4. **Cell communication**     | Allows interaction with other cells                      |
| 5. **Protection**             | Protects the internal contents of the cell               |

#### How Molecules Pass Through:

| Molecule Type                        | Can Pass?      | How?                                               |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hydrophobic (nonpolar)** molecules | ✅ Yes, easily | Dissolve in lipid bilayer and pass through rapidly |
| **Hydrophilic (polar)** molecules    | ❌ Not easily  | Need transport proteins to help them cross         |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> The plasma membrane is like a security gate — it decides what goes in and out of the cell. It's made of a double layer of fats (phospholipids) with protein guards.

### 📝 Exam Style Answer

The plasma membrane is a phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins.
It is selectively permeable and controls movement of substances into and out of the cell.

Functions:

- Forms boundary of the cell
- Regulates transport
- Enables communication
- Protects internal contents

Summary: Plasma membrane maintains internal balance and controls exchange with the environment.
