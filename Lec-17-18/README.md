### 2) Heart and Blood Flow Path

| Flow             | English                                                                           | বাংলা                                                                     |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pulmonary return | Lungs -> Pulmonary vein -> Left atrium -> Left ventricle -> Aorta -> Body         | ফুসফুস -> পালমোনারি ভেইন -> বাম অলিন্দ -> বাম নিলয় -> এওর্টা -> দেহ       |
| Systemic return  | Body -> Vena cava -> Right atrium -> Right ventricle -> Pulmonary artery -> Lungs | দেহ -> ভেনা কাভা -> ডান অলিন্দ -> ডান নিলয় -> পালমোনারি আর্টারি -> ফুসফুস |

### 3) Systole and Diastole

| Term     | English                                    | বাংলা                                        |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| Diastole | Heart relaxes and chambers fill with blood | হৃদপিণ্ড শিথিল থাকে, প্রকোষ্ঠ রক্তে পূর্ণ হয় |
| Systole  | Heart contracts and ejects blood           | হৃদপিণ্ড সংকুচিত হয়ে রক্ত পাম্প করে          |

### 4) Artery vs Vein vs Capillary

| Feature        | Artery            | Vein         | Capillary                |
| -------------- | ----------------- | ------------ | ------------------------ |
| Direction      | Away from heart   | Toward heart | Connects artery and vein |
| Pressure       | High              | Low          | Very low                 |
| Wall thickness | Thick and elastic | Thinner      | One-cell thick           |
| Key role       | Fast distribution | Return flow  | Exchange with tissues    |

### 5) Topic 17 Side Notes and Common Mistakes

### Exam Tips

- Remember: Right -> Lungs, Left -> Body.
- Pulmonary artery is an exception: it carries deoxygenated blood.
- Pulmonary vein is an exception: it carries oxygenated blood.

### Common Mistakes

- Mistake: "All arteries carry oxygenated blood." Not always true.
- Mistake: "All veins carry deoxygenated blood." Not always true.
- Mistake: Mixing systemic vs pulmonary circuits.

### 6) Solved Examples (Topic 17)

Example 1: Trace blood after it leaves the right ventricle.

- Right ventricle -> Pulmonary artery -> Lungs -> Pulmonary vein -> Left atrium.
  Final answer: It goes to lungs for oxygenation, then returns to left atrium.

Example 2: Why are artery walls thicker than vein walls?

- Arteries face high pressure from heart pumping.
- They need elastic, muscular walls to tolerate pressure pulse.
  Final answer: Structural adaptation to high pressure.

### 7) MCQ Practice (Topic 17)

1. The chamber that pumps blood to the lungs is:
   A) Left atrium B) Left ventricle C) Right atrium D) Right ventricle  
   Answer: D  
   Why: Right ventricle pumps into pulmonary artery.

2. Which vessel carries blood from lungs to heart?
   A) Aorta B) Pulmonary vein C) Vena cava D) Pulmonary artery  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Pulmonary vein returns oxygenated blood to left atrium.

3. Capillaries are specialized for:
   A) High-pressure pumping B) Valve function C) Diffusion and exchange D) Hemoglobin synthesis  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Their thin wall allows exchange.

4. During systole, the heart:
   A) Relaxes B) Contracts C) Stops beating D) Absorbs nutrients  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Systole = contraction phase.

5. Which is true about veins?
   A) Thickest wall B) Highest pressure C) One-way valves in many veins D) Carry blood away from heart  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Valves prevent backflow in low-pressure return system.

6. Systemic circulation is between:
   A) Heart and lungs B) Heart and body tissues C) Lungs and body tissues D) Liver and pancreas  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Systemic circuit serves whole body.

7. Pulmonary circulation uses mainly:
   A) Left half of heart B) Right half of heart C) Both halves equally at once D) Only atria  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Right side pumps blood to lungs.

8. Atherosclerosis increases risk of:
   A) Better oxygen delivery B) Stroke and heart attack C) Improved immunity D) Lower blood pressure only  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Plaques narrow arteries and reduce blood flow.

### 5) Topic 18 Side Notes and Common Mistakes

### Exam Tips

- Plasma proportion question: usually around 55%.
- RBC hallmark: hemoglobin + biconcave flexible shape.
- Platelet count and clotting sequence are common MCQ themes.
- LDL is often called bad cholesterol; HDL is relatively protective.

### Common Mistakes

- Mistake: "WBC carry oxygen." Correct: RBC carry oxygen.
- Mistake: "Platelets are full cells." Correct: They are cell fragments.
- Mistake: Confusing antigen and antibody during blood group questions.

### 6) Solved Examples (Topic 18)

Example 1: A patient has severe bleeding. Which blood component is most directly needed?

- Core defect is clot formation.
- Platelets are central for initial plug and clot cascade triggering.
  Final answer: Platelets are primarily needed.

Example 2: Why can O- be given to many recipients?

- O- RBCs lack A, B, and Rh antigens.
- Lower chance of immediate antigen-antibody reaction in recipient.
  Final answer: O- is considered universal donor in standard transfusion MCQs.

## Final Quick Revision (17-18)

- Heart is a 4-chamber pump: RA, RV, LA, LV.
- Right side -> lungs (pulmonary); left side -> body (systemic).
- Arteries away, veins return, capillaries exchange.
- Atherosclerosis raises risk of heart attack and stroke.
- Blood = plasma + formed elements (RBC, WBC, platelets).
- ABO and Rh compatibility are critical in transfusion.
