Some common  vegetable oils  are peanut, soybean, and corn oil.   Waxes   Both plants and animals produce waxes. The  waxy coating  on some plants leaves is an example of plant waxes. 33 DNA RNA Structural Name: Deoxyribonucleic Acid Ribonucleic Acid Function: Medium of long-term storage and transmission of genetic information Transfer the genetic code needed for the creation of proteins from the nucleus to the ribosome. Without RNA, proteins could never be made. Structure: Typically a double- stranded molecule with a long chain of nucleotides. A single-stranded molecule in most of its biological roles and has a shorter chain of nucleotides. Bases/Sugars:  Long polymer with a  deoxyribose  and phosphate backbone and four different bases: adenine, guanine, cytosine and thymine. Shorter polymer with a ribose and phosphate backbone and four different bases: adenine, guanine, cytosine, and  uracil . Base Pairing: A-T (Adenine-Thymine), G-C (Guanine-Cytosine) A-U (Adenine- Uracil ), G-C (Guanine-Cytosine)
