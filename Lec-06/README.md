## Q1: Describe the structure of an atom and explain the roles of protons, neutrons, and electrons.

### ✅ Structure of an Atom

> An **atom** is the smallest particle of an element that has the characteristics of that element. Atoms are the **basic building blocks of all matter**.

---

### 🧬 Parts of an Atom

An atom has **three basic parts:**

| Particle     | Charge       | Location                                           | Role                                                   |
| ------------ | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Proton**   | Positive (+) | Inside the **nucleus**                             | Determines the identity of the element (atomic number) |
| **Neutron**  | Neutral (0)  | Inside the **nucleus**                             | Adds mass and stability to the nucleus                 |
| **Electron** | Negative (−) | Orbiting the nucleus in **energy levels/orbitals** | Involved in chemical bonding and reactions             |

---

### 🔑 Key Points

- The **center** of an atom is called the **nucleus** — it contains protons and neutrons
- **Atomic mass** = Protons + Neutrons
- **Electrons** orbit the nucleus in energy levels (electron cloud)
- Because **opposites attract**, the negative electrons are pulled toward the positive nucleus
- If the number of **electrons = protons**, the atom is **neutral** (no charge)

---

### 📊 Electron Energy Levels

| Energy Level | Maximum Electrons      |
| ------------ | ---------------------- |
| 1st level    | **2** electrons        |
| 2nd level    | **8** electrons        |
| 3rd level    | Up to **18** electrons |
| 4th level    | Up to **32** electrons |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> An atom is like a tiny solar system — the nucleus (sun) is in the center with protons and neutrons, and electrons (planets) spin around it in different energy levels.

### 📝 Exam Style Answer

An atom consists of a central nucleus containing protons and neutrons, with electrons arranged in energy levels around it.

Proton: Positively charged particle; determines atomic number and identity of the element.  
Neutron: Neutral particle; contributes to atomic mass and nuclear stability.  
Electron: Negatively charged particle in energy levels; involved in bonding and chemical reactions.

Summary: Atom = nucleus (protons + neutrons) with electrons around it.

## Q2: Explain the concept of atomic number and atomic mass and describe how isotopes differ from each other.

### ✅ Atomic Number and Atomic Mass

| Concept                       | Definition                                            | Formula                           |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Atomic Number**             | Number of **protons** in the nucleus                  | Atomic Number = Number of Protons |
| **Atomic Mass** (Mass Number) | Total number of **protons + neutrons** in the nucleus | Atomic Mass = Protons + Neutrons  |

👉 **Example (Carbon):**

- Atomic number = 6 (6 protons)
- Atomic mass = 12 (6 protons + 6 neutrons)

---

### ✅ Isotopes

> **Isotopes** are atoms of the **same element** that have the **same number of protons** but **different numbers of neutrons**.

- If the neutron number changes → the **atomic mass also changes**
- The **proton number stays the same** (because it's the same element)

---

### 📊 Isotopes of Carbon — Example

| Isotope             | Protons | Neutrons | Atomic Mass | Stable?                   |
| ------------------- | ------- | -------- | ----------- | ------------------------- |
| **Carbon-12 (¹²C)** | 6       | 6        | 12          | ✅ Stable                 |
| **Carbon-13 (¹³C)** | 6       | 7        | 13          | ✅ Stable                 |
| **Carbon-14 (¹⁴C)** | 6       | 8        | 14          | ❌ Radioactive (unstable) |

---

### ✅ Radioisotopes

- A **radioisotope** is an isotope with an **unstable nucleus**
- **Radioactive decay**: the process where the nucleus spontaneously disintegrates, emitting energy and subatomic particles
- This process can **transform one element into another**

👉 **Example:** ¹⁴C (Carbon-14) decays — one neutron splits into a proton and electron → becomes ¹⁴N (Nitrogen-14)

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Isotopes are like siblings — same family (same element, same protons) but different weight (different neutrons). Some unstable ones are "radioactive."

### 📝 Exam Style Answer

Atomic number: Number of protons in the nucleus.  
Atomic mass (mass number): Protons + neutrons.

Isotopes are atoms of the same element with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons, so their masses differ.

Summary: Same protons = same element; different neutrons = different isotope.

## Q3: Explain how chemical bonds are formed and describe the characteristics of ionic and covalent bonds.

### ✅ How Chemical Bonds are Formed

> Chemical bonds form when atoms **share or transfer electrons** to become more stable.

- Electrons in the **highest energy level** are called **valence electrons**
- Valence electrons decide **how an atom bonds** with others
- Atoms are happiest (most stable) with a **full outer shell**
- When atoms move or share electrons, they get **linked together** — this is **chemical bonding**

---

### Three Main Types of Chemical Bonds

---

### 1. ⚡ Ionic Bond

| Feature          | Detail                                                                                                               |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **How it forms** | **Transfer** of electrons from one atom to another                                                                   |
| **Found in**     | Mainly **inorganic** compounds                                                                                       |
| **Result**       | One atom loses electrons (becomes **positive ion/cation**), another gains electrons (becomes **negative ion/anion**) |
| **Example**      | NaCl (table salt) — Na gives electron to Cl                                                                          |

👉 **Related concepts:**

- **Oxidation** = loss of electrons
- **Reduction** = gain of electrons
- **Redox reaction** = reactions where electrons are gained or lost

---

### 2. 🤝 Covalent Bond

| Feature          | Detail                                               |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **How it forms** | **Sharing** of a pair of electrons between two atoms |
| **Found in**     | Mainly **organic** compounds                         |
| **Result**       | Both atoms share electrons equally or unequally      |
| **Example**      | H₂O (water), CH₄ (methane), O₂                       |

---

### 3. 💧 Hydrogen Bond

| Feature          | Detail                                                     |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **How it forms** | When hydrogen is attracted to another electronegative atom |
| **Strength**     | Weaker than ionic and covalent bonds                       |
| **Found in**     | Water molecules, DNA structure                             |

---

### 🔑 Comparison Table

| Feature             | Ionic Bond ⚡       | Covalent Bond 🤝  |
| ------------------- | ------------------- | ----------------- |
| **Electron action** | Transfer            | Sharing           |
| **Found in**        | Inorganic compounds | Organic compounds |
| **Strength**        | Strong              | Strong            |
| **Example**         | NaCl                | H₂O, CO₂          |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Ionic = one gives, one takes. Covalent = both share. Hydrogen = weak attraction between molecules.

### 📝 Exam Style Answer

Chemical bonds are formed when atoms transfer or share valence electrons to become stable.

Ionic bond: Electron transfer from one atom to another, producing oppositely charged ions (e.g., NaCl).  
Covalent bond: Electron sharing between atoms (e.g., H2O, CH4).

Summary: Ionic = transfer; Covalent = sharing.

## Q6: Describe the difference between polar and nonpolar molecules with examples.

### ✅ Polar vs. Nonpolar Molecules

> When electrons in a covalent bond are **not equally shared**, the molecule is **polar**.

---

### Polar Molecules

- Electrons are **shared unequally** between atoms
- One side of the molecule has a **slight negative charge**, the other a **slight positive charge**
- Dissolve in water (hydrophilic)

👉 **Example:** **Water (H₂O)** — oxygen pulls electrons more strongly than hydrogen, making it polar

---

### Nonpolar Molecules

- Electrons are **shared equally** between atoms
- No partial charges on the molecule
- Do **not** dissolve in water (hydrophobic)

👉 **Example:** **Oils, fats, O₂, CH₄** — electrons are shared equally

---

### 🔑 Comparison Table

| Feature                 | Polar Molecule            | Nonpolar Molecule  |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------ |
| **Electron sharing**    | Unequal                   | Equal              |
| **Charge distribution** | Has partial charges (+/−) | No partial charges |
| **Dissolves in water?** | Yes (hydrophilic)         | No (hydrophobic)   |
| **Example**             | Water (H₂O)               | Oil, O₂, CH₄       |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Polar = uneven sharing (like two friends sharing candy unfairly). Nonpolar = even sharing (exactly 50-50).

### 📝 Exam Style Answer

Polar molecules form when electrons are shared unequally, creating partial charges (e.g., water).  
Nonpolar molecules form when electrons are shared equally, with no partial charges (e.g., oils, O2).

Summary: Polar = unequal sharing and partial charges; Nonpolar = equal sharing and no partial charges.
