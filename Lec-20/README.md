## 2) Normal Glucose Metabolism

| Step | English Explanation                              | বাংলা ব্যাখ্যা                                    |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | After a meal, blood glucose rises.               | খাবারের পরে রক্তে গ্লুকোজ বাড়ে।                   |
| 2    | The pancreas releases insulin.                   | অগ্ন্যাশয় ইনসুলিন নিঃসরণ করে।                     |
| 3    | Muscle and fat cells absorb glucose from blood.  | পেশি ও চর্বি কোষ রক্ত থেকে গ্লুকোজ গ্রহণ করে।     |
| 4    | The liver converts excess glucose into glycogen. | যকৃত অতিরিক্ত গ্লুকোজকে গ্লাইকোজেনে রূপান্তর করে। |
| 5    | Blood glucose falls back toward normal level.    | রক্তে গ্লুকোজের মাত্রা স্বাভাবিকের দিকে ফিরে আসে। |

### Key Relationship

- Low / no insulin -> less glucose enters cells -> blood glucose rises -> hyperglycemia

## 6) Side Notes, Shortcuts, and Common Mistakes

### High-Yield Tips

- Insulin lowers blood glucose by promoting uptake and storage.
- Type 1 usually means little or no insulin production.
- Type 2 usually means insulin resistance.
- HDL is good, LDL is bad for exam MCQs.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Mistake: "Insulin raises blood glucose." Correct: Insulin lowers blood glucose.
- Mistake: "All diabetes cases are Type 1." Correct: Most cases are Type 2.
- Mistake: "Gestational diabetes has no effect on the baby." Correct: It can cause several fetal and neonatal complications.
- Mistake: "High HDL is dangerous." Correct: Higher HDL is usually protective.

## 7) Solved Examples

### Example 1: Why does blood glucose remain high in diabetes?

- In diabetes, insulin may be absent, too low, or ineffective.
- Cells cannot absorb enough glucose from blood.
- Glucose accumulates in the bloodstream.

Final answer: Blood glucose remains high because insulin action is inadequate, so glucose is not taken up properly by body cells.

### Example 2: A patient has high LDL and low HDL. What does this suggest?

- High LDL increases plaque formation risk.
- Low HDL reduces cholesterol removal from blood.

Final answer: This lipid pattern suggests increased cardiovascular risk.

## 8) MCQ Practice (Lec 20)

1. The hormone that lowers blood glucose is:
   A) Thyroxine B) Insulin C) Adrenaline D) Glucagon  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Insulin promotes glucose uptake and storage.

2. Hyperglycemia means:
   A) Low blood glucose B) High blood pressure C) High blood glucose D) Low insulin only  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Hyperglycemia is the condition of excess glucose in blood.

3. Type 1 diabetes mainly occurs because of:
   A) Excess insulin B) Autoimmune destruction of beta cells C) High HDL D) Excess glycogen  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Beta-cell destruction leads to little or no insulin production.

4. Which form of diabetes appears during pregnancy?
   A) Type 1 B) Type 2 C) Gestational diabetes D) Juvenile diabetes  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Gestational diabetes is diagnosed for the first time during pregnancy.

5. Which cholesterol is considered protective?
   A) LDL B) VLDL C) HDL D) Triglyceride  
   Answer: C  
   Why: HDL helps transport cholesterol back to the liver.

6. The organ that secretes insulin is:
   A) Liver B) Pancreas C) Kidney D) Gallbladder  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Beta cells of the pancreas produce insulin.

7. High triglycerides mainly indicate high levels of:
   A) Protein B) Stored fat C) Vitamins D) Hemoglobin  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Triglycerides are the main storage form of fat.

8. Which statement is correct?
   A) LDL is the good cholesterol
   B) Insulin resistance is common in Type 2 diabetes
   C) Gestational diabetes never affects the baby
   D) Hyperglycemia means low blood sugar  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Type 2 diabetes is strongly linked with insulin resistance.
