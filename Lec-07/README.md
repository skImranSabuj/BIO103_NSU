## Q1: Explain the difference between monomers and polymers and describe the processes of condensation and hydrolysis reactions.

### ✅ Monomers vs. Polymers

| Term        | Definition                                                                         | Example                           |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Monomer** | Small molecules or **building blocks** that join together to form larger molecules | Glucose, Amino acids, Nucleotides |
| **Polymer** | Large molecules (macromolecules) made of **repeating units** of monomers           | Cellulose, Proteins, DNA          |

> **Macromolecule:** Small molecules join together in different ways to make very large molecules.

👉 **Example:** Glucose molecules (monomers) assemble to make cellulose (polymer — a carbohydrate)

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Monomers = individual beads. Polymer = a necklace made from beads.

---

### ✅ Condensation Reaction (Building Up = Anabolism)

> **Polymerization** is the linking together of monomers to form polymers.

| Feature          | Detail                                         |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **What happens** | Monomers are joined together to form a polymer |
| **Water**        | A small molecule (water) is **released/lost**  |
| **Energy**       | **Requires input** of energy                   |
| **Also called**  | Dehydration synthesis                          |

👉 **Example:** Amino acids joined by condensation → Protein

---

### ✅ Hydrolysis Reaction (Breaking Down = Catabolism)

> **Hydrolysis** is the **reverse of condensation** — it breaks apart large molecules into smaller ones.

| Feature          | Detail                                                            |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **What happens** | Polymer is broken into monomers                                   |
| **Water**        | Water is **used/added** to break bonds                            |
| **Energy**       | **Releases energy** (liberates energy stored during condensation) |

👉 **Example:** Starch + Water → Glucose molecules

---

### 🔑 Comparison Table

| Feature     | Condensation 🧱       | Hydrolysis 🔥    |
| ----------- | --------------------- | ---------------- |
| **Action**  | Builds polymers       | Breaks polymers  |
| **Water**   | Released              | Used             |
| **Energy**  | Input needed          | Energy released  |
| **Type**    | Anabolism             | Catabolism       |
| **Example** | Amino acids → Protein | Starch → Glucose |

💡 **Link to Metabolism:**

> Condensation = Anabolism (building). Hydrolysis = Catabolism (breaking).

## Q2: Describe the structure and biological functions of carbohydrates.

### ✅ Structure of Carbohydrates

| Feature           | Detail                                          |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Common symbol** | (CH₂O)ₙ                                         |
| **Elements**      | Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen in **1:2:1 ratio** |
| **Most common**   | Glucose: C₆H₁₂O₆                                |

---

### ✅ Three Types of Carbohydrates

#### 1. Monosaccharides (Simple Sugars)

- Single sugar unit — the **monomer** of carbohydrates
- 👉 Examples: **Glucose**, Fructose, Galactose

#### 2. Disaccharides (Double Sugars)

- Two monosaccharides joined together

| Disaccharide | Made From           | Found In                 |
| ------------ | ------------------- | ------------------------ |
| **Sucrose**  | Glucose + Fructose  | Table sugar              |
| **Lactose**  | Glucose + Galactose | Milk sugar               |
| **Maltose**  | Glucose + Glucose   | Barley/germinating seeds |

#### 3. Polysaccharides (Complex Carbohydrates)

- Long chains of monosaccharide units bonded together
- 👉 Examples: **Starch, Cellulose, Glycogen**

---

### ✅ Functions of Carbohydrates

| Function                      | Explanation                                            |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1. **Providing energy**       | Main source of energy for cells                        |
| 2. **Energy storage**         | Stored as glycogen (animals) or starch (plants)        |
| 3. **Structural support**     | Cellulose in plant cell walls                          |
| 4. **Sparing protein**        | Prevents protein from being used for energy            |
| 5. **Biological recognition** | Cell surface carbohydrates help in cell identification |
| 6. **Dietary fiber**          | Essential for elimination of waste and toxins          |
| 7. **Flavor and sweeteners**  | Sugars give sweet taste                                |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Carbohydrates = sugars and starches = quick energy food for cells!

## Q3: Describe the structure of proteins and explain the role of amino acids as building blocks.

### ✅ Structure of Proteins

> **Proteins** are the **polymers of amino acids** — the most diverse molecules in both structure and function.

- Thousands of different proteins are made from only **20 amino acids** (monomers)
- Amino acids are bonded together by **peptide bonds** to form proteins

---

### ✅ Amino Acid Structure

Each amino acid contains:

| Component                 | Description                                      |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Amine group (NH₃)**     | Basic / positive                                 |
| **Carboxyl group (COOH)** | Acidic / negative                                |
| **R group**               | Variable group — different for each amino acid   |
| **Central carbon**        | All three groups are attached to the same carbon |

---

### Two Types of Amino Acids

| Type              | Detail                                                  |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Essential**     | Must be supplied in the **diet** (body can't make them) |
| **Non-essential** | Body can make them (not required from diet)             |

---

### ✅ Peptide Bonds

> The bond that forms between two amino acids is called a **peptide bond**.

- Two amino acids → Dipeptide
- Many amino acids → Polypeptide → Protein

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Amino acids are like LEGO bricks. Different combinations of 20 types make thousands of different proteins.

---

## Q4: Explain the four levels of protein structure with examples.

### ✅ Four Levels of Protein Structure

| Level   | Name                     | Description                                                                                   | Example                                |
| ------- | ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **1st** | **Primary Structure**    | The **linear arrangement** (sequence) of amino acids in a protein — like letters in a word    | Insulin                                |
| **2nd** | **Secondary Structure**  | Areas of **folding or coiling** within a protein                                              | **α-helices** and **β-pleated sheets** |
| **3rd** | **Tertiary Structure**   | Final **3D shape** of a protein, resulting from non-covalent interactions between amino acids | Myoglobin                              |
| **4th** | **Quaternary Structure** | Non-covalent interactions that bind **multiple polypeptides** into a single, larger protein   | **Hemoglobin** (4 polypeptide chains)  |

---

### 🔄 Visual Flow

```
Primary → Secondary → Tertiary → Quaternary
(Sequence) → (Folding) → (3D Shape) → (Multiple chains)
```

---

### ✅ Main Functions of Proteins

| Function               | Explanation                              |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Build and repair**   | Build, maintain, and repair body tissues |
| **Energy source**      | Can be used for energy                   |
| **Storage**            | Store food and energy                    |
| **Enzymes**            | Catalyze biochemical reactions           |
| **Genetic expression** | Express genetic information              |
| **Antibodies**         | Prevent disease (immune function)        |
| **Growth**             | Milk proteins help infant growth         |

💡 **Easy idea:**

> Primary = sequence of beads on a string. Secondary = string coils or folds. Tertiary = whole string crumpled into a ball. Quaternary = multiple balls joined together.

## 📝 Exam Style Answers (Concise)

### Q1: Differentiate monomers and polymers; explain condensation and hydrolysis.

Monomers are small repeating units, while polymers are large molecules formed by many monomers. Condensation (dehydration synthesis) joins monomers by removing water and requires energy. Hydrolysis breaks polymers into monomers by adding water and releases usable energy.

### Q2: Describe carbohydrate structure and functions.

Carbohydrates are composed of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen in approximately 1:2:1 ratio. They occur as monosaccharides, disaccharides, and polysaccharides, and function in rapid energy supply, energy storage, structural support (cellulose), and biological recognition.

### Q3: Describe protein structure and role of amino acids.

Proteins are polymers made of amino acids linked by peptide bonds. Amino acids serve as fundamental building blocks, and variation in amino acid sequence produces proteins with different structures and functions.

### Q4: Explain the four levels of protein structure.

Primary structure is the amino acid sequence. Secondary structure involves local folding into alpha-helices or beta-sheets. Tertiary structure is the overall 3D conformation of one polypeptide. Quaternary structure is the association of multiple polypeptide chains into one functional protein.
