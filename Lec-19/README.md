## 2) Cellular Respiration vs Breathing

| Feature | Cellular Respiration | Breathing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Definition | Biochemical process that releases energy from food inside cells. | Mechanical process of moving air into and out of the lungs. |
| বাংলা সংজ্ঞা | কোষের ভেতরে খাদ্য ভেঙে শক্তি মুক্ত করার জৈব-রাসায়নিক প্রক্রিয়া। | ফুসফুসে বায়ু ঢোকানো ও বের করার যান্ত্রিক প্রক্রিয়া। |
| Location | Inside cells, mainly in mitochondria. | In respiratory organs, mainly lungs. |
| Main outcome | Produces ATP, carbon dioxide, and water. | Supplies oxygen and removes carbon dioxide. |
| Nature | Chemical process | Physical process |

### Key Formula

- Aerobic respiration: Glucose + Oxygen -> Carbon dioxide + Water + Energy (ATP)

## 5) Side Notes, Shortcuts, and Common Mistakes

### High-Yield Tips

- Breathing and respiration are related but not identical terms.
- Alveoli are adapted for diffusion because they are thin, moist, and numerous.
- Oxygen travels from lungs to blood; carbon dioxide travels from blood to lungs.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Mistake: "Breathing and cellular respiration are the same." Correct: Breathing is physical; cellular respiration is biochemical.
- Mistake: "Gas exchange occurs in the trachea." Correct: It mainly occurs in alveoli.
- Mistake: "Oxygen diffuses from blood to alveoli." Correct: In normal lungs, oxygen diffuses from alveoli to blood.

## 6) Solved Examples

### Example 1: Why are alveoli efficient for gas exchange?

- They are very numerous, so total surface area is high.
- Their walls are one cell thick.
- They are surrounded by dense capillary networks.

Final answer: Alveoli are efficient because they provide a large, thin, well-supplied surface for rapid diffusion.

### Example 2: Trace the path of oxygen from outside air to blood.

- Nose -> Pharynx -> Larynx -> Trachea -> Bronchi -> Bronchioles -> Alveoli
- Oxygen then diffuses into surrounding capillaries.

Final answer: Oxygen moves through the respiratory tract and finally diffuses from alveoli into capillary blood.

## 7) MCQ Practice (Lec 19)

1. The main site of gas exchange is:
   A) Trachea B) Bronchus C) Alveolus D) Pharynx  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Alveoli are specialized for diffusion of gases.

2. Breathing is best described as:
   A) ATP production B) A physical movement of air C) Digestion of food D) Blood clotting  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Breathing is the mechanical movement of air in and out of the lungs.

3. Which gas diffuses from blood into alveoli?
   A) Nitrogen B) Oxygen C) Carbon dioxide D) Helium  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Blood carries CO2 to lungs for removal.

4. The correct air pathway starts with:
   A) Trachea B) Larynx C) Nose D) Bronchiole  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Air normally enters through the nose first.

5. Cellular respiration mainly occurs in:
   A) Alveoli B) Mitochondria C) Trachea D) Plasma  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Mitochondria are the main sites of aerobic respiration.

6. The respiratory system works most directly with the:
   A) Digestive system B) Skeletal system C) Circulatory system D) Reproductive system  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Blood carries respiratory gases between lungs and tissues.

7. Which statement is true?
   A) Oxygen diffuses from blood to alveoli in a healthy lung
   B) Alveoli have thick walls for protection
   C) Carbon dioxide is a useful nutrient
   D) Alveoli have thin walls to help diffusion  
   Answer: D  
   Why: Thin walls shorten diffusion distance.
