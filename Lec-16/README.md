## 2) Human Digestive Tract Flow (Very Important)

Mouth -> Pharynx -> Esophagus -> Stomach -> Small Intestine -> Large Intestine -> Rectum -> Anus

| Organ                                      | Key function                                                             | বাংলা সারাংশ                                         |
| ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Mouth (teeth + tongue + saliva)            | Chewing, bolus formation, starch digestion starts by salivary amylase    | চিবানো, বলাস তৈরি, অ্যামাইলেজ দিয়ে স্টার্চ ভাঙা শুরু |
| Esophagus                                  | Peristalsis moves food to stomach                                        | Peristalsis দিয়ে খাদ্য পাকস্থলীতে যায়                |
| Stomach                                    | Acidic digestion (HCl), pepsin starts protein digestion, chyme formation | HCl ও pepsin প্রোটিন ভাঙে, chyme তৈরি হয়             |
| Small intestine (duodenum, jejunum, ileum) | Major chemical digestion and nutrient absorption                         | বেশিরভাগ রাসায়নিক পরিপাক ও শোষণ এখানে                |
| Villi + microvilli                         | Increase surface area for absorption                                     | শোষণের ক্ষেত্রফল অনেক বাড়ায়                          |
| Large intestine                            | Water reabsorption and feces formation                                   | পানি শোষণ ও মল তৈরি                                  |
| Rectum and anus                            | Storage and elimination of feces                                         | মল জমা ও নির্গমন                                     |

## 5) Side Notes, Shortcuts, and Common Mistakes

### High-yield Tips

- Shortcut order: M-E-S-S-L-R-A (Mouth, Esophagus, Stomach, Small intestine, Large intestine, Rectum, Anus)
- pH fact: Stomach is highly acidic (about pH 2), needed to activate pepsin.
- Most absorption happens in small intestine (especially ileum), not in stomach.
- Villi and microvilli are favorite MCQ points.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Mistake: "Bile is an enzyme." Correct: Bile salts help fat emulsification; bile is not a digestive enzyme.
- Mistake: "Most digestion happens in large intestine." Correct: Most digestion/absorption occurs in small intestine.
- Mistake: "Food passes through liver/gallbladder/pancreas." Correct: They are accessory; food does not pass through them.
- Mistake: "Pepsin is secreted directly in active form." Correct: Chief cells secrete pepsinogen; activated in acidic environment.

## 6) Solved Examples

### Example 1: What happens to a bite of bread in early digestion?

1. Bread enters mouth; chewing increases surface area.
2. Salivary amylase starts starch digestion.
3. Bolus moves by peristalsis through esophagus.
4. In stomach, acid and churning continue digestion.
5. In small intestine, pancreatic amylase completes carbohydrate digestion.

Final answer: Mechanical + chemical digestion starts in mouth; major completion and absorption occur in small intestine.

### Example 2: Why does low stomach pH matter?

- HCl lowers pH to around 2.
- This converts pepsinogen to pepsin.
- Pepsin digests proteins efficiently.

Final answer: Acidic pH is essential for activating pepsin and starting protein digestion.

## 7) MCQ Practice (Topic 16)

1. The major site of nutrient absorption is:
   A) Stomach B) Small intestine C) Large intestine D) Esophagus  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Villi and microvilli in small intestine maximize absorption.

2. Which organ stores bile?
   A) Liver B) Pancreas C) Gallbladder D) Duodenum  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Liver makes bile; gallbladder stores/concentrates it.

3. Peristalsis is best defined as:
   A) Hormone secretion B) Rhythmic muscular contraction C) Enzyme activation D) Bile release  
   Answer: B  
   Why: It propels food along digestive tube.

4. Pepsinogen is secreted by:
   A) Parietal cells B) Chief cells C) Goblet cells D) Hepatocytes  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Chief cells release pepsinogen; parietal cells release HCl.

5. Which one is NOT an accessory digestive organ?
   A) Liver B) Pancreas C) Gallbladder D) Stomach  
   Answer: D  
   Why: Stomach is part of alimentary canal.

6. Bicarbonate in pancreatic juice mainly:
   A) Digests fats B) Neutralizes acidic chyme C) Absorbs glucose D) Activates bile pigments  
   Answer: B  
   Why: It raises pH in duodenum.

7. Villi and microvilli mainly function to:
   A) Produce HCl B) Increase absorption area C) Store bile D) Move feces  
   Answer: B  
   Why: Larger area means better nutrient uptake.

8. The final exit of undigested waste is through:
   A) Ileum B) Rectum C) Anus D) Duodenum  
   Answer: C  
   Why: Rectum stores; anus expels feces.

## 8) 60-Second Revision Block

- Digestion = mechanical + chemical breakdown of food.
- Small intestine = main digestion + absorption center.
- Stomach: HCl + pepsin for protein digestion.
- Liver makes bile; gallbladder stores bile; pancreas sends enzymes + bicarbonate.
- Large intestine mainly compacts waste and reabsorbs water.
