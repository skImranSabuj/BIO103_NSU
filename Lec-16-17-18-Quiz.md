# 📝 BIO103 MCQ Practice (Lectures 16–18)

## Topic 16: Digestion

### 1. Digestion is fundamentally defined as:
A) Physical breakdown of food only  
B) Chemical breakdown of food only  
C) Mechanical and chemical breakdown into absorbable molecules  
D) Movement of food through the tract  

**Answer: C**

---

### 2. Which of the following is NOT a macromolecule found in food?
A) Carbohydrates  
B) Proteins  
C) Vitamins  
D) Nucleic acids  

**Answer: C**

---

### 3. Herbivores have digestive systems specially adapted to:
A) Break down meat  
B) Break down plant material  
C) Absorb fats  
D) Produce bile  

**Answer: B**

---

### 4. An omnivore's digestive system is suited for:
A) Plant material only  
B) Meat only  
C) Both plant and animal material  
D) Liquids only  

**Answer: C**

---

### 5. Carnivores like eagles and wolves primarily digest:
A) Fruits and seeds  
B) Meat and bone  
C) Leaves  
D) Fungi  

**Answer: B**

---

### 6. The human digestive system consists of:
A) Only the mouth and stomach  
B) Alimentary canal and accessory organs  
C) Liver and pancreas only  
D) Only the small intestine  

**Answer: B**

---

### 7. Food passes through the digestive system in which direction?
A) Mouth → Anus only  
B) Anus → Mouth  
C) Random pattern  
D) Mouth → Pharynx → Stomach → Anus  

**Answer: D**

---

### 8. Accessory digestive organs are defined as organs that:
A) Food passes directly through  
B) Aid digestion but food does not pass through them  
C) Absorb most nutrients  
D) Move waste out of the body  

**Answer: B**

---

### 9. The total length of the human alimentary canal is approximately:
A) 2 meters  
B) 5–7 meters  
C) 10 meters  
D) 15 meters  

**Answer: B**

---

### 10. Which organ is considered part of the alimentary canal?
A) Liver  
B) Pancreas  
C) Esophagus  
D) Gallbladder  

**Answer: C**

---

### 11. The major enzyme secreted in saliva is:
A) Pepsin  
B) Lipase  
C) Salivary amylase  
D) Trypsin  

**Answer: C**

---

### 12. Salivary amylase breaks down:
A) Proteins  
B) Fats  
C) Starch  
D) Nucleic acids  

**Answer: C**

---

### 13. A bolus is best described as:
A) An enzyme in the mouth  
B) A hormone that increases appetite  
C) A rounded, easy-to-swallow ball of food formed in the mouth  
D) Partially digested food from the stomach  

**Answer: C**

---

### 14. The tongue's primary functions in digestion include:
A) Secreting saliva only  
B) Manipulating food and forming bolus  
C) Breaking down proteins  
D) Producing stomach acid  

**Answer: B**

---

### 15. Saliva contains all the following EXCEPT:
A) Water  
B) Salt  
C) Salivary amylase  
D) Pepsin  

**Answer: D**

---

### 16. Buffers in saliva serve to:
A) Speed up digestion  
B) Neutralize acidic foods  
C) Kill bacteria  
D) Lubricate food  

**Answer: B**

---

### 17. Antibacterial substances in saliva mainly:
A) Digest carbohydrates  
B) Kill bacteria in food  
C) Absorb nutrients  
D) Produce acid  

**Answer: B**

---

### 18. The pharynx connects the mouth to:
A) Stomach  
B) Esophagus  
C) Small intestine  
D) Duodenum  

**Answer: B**

---

### 19. Peristalsis is best defined as:
A) Hormone secretion  
B) Rhythmic muscular contraction that moves food  
C) Enzyme activation  
D) Bile release  

**Answer: B**

---

### 20. Sphincter muscles in the esophagus prevent:
A) Swallowing  
B) Backflow of food  
C) Enzyme production  
D) Saliva secretion  

**Answer: B**

---

## Topic 17: Circulatory System

### 21. The circulatory system consists of:
A) Heart and blood only  
B) Blood and blood vessels only  
C) Heart, blood, and blood vessels  
D) Heart and lungs only  

**Answer: C**

---

### 22. The primary functions of the circulatory system include:
A) Transport of oxygen and nutrients only  
B) Removal of wastes only  
C) Transport, waste removal, immunity, clotting, temperature regulation  
D) Digestion only  

**Answer: C**

---

### 23. The heart is divided into how many chambers?
A) 2  
B) 3  
C) 4  
D) 5  

**Answer: C**

---

### 24. The primary function of the atria is to:
A) Pump blood out of the heart  
B) Collect blood returning to the heart  
C) Produce red blood cells  
D) Filter blood  

**Answer: B**

---

### 25. Arteries carry blood:
A) Away from the heart  
B) Toward the heart  
C) Between capillaries  
D) Only through lungs  

**Answer: A**

---

### 26. Veins carry blood:
A) Away from the heart  
B) Toward the heart  
C) Throughout the body  
D) Only in the lungs  

**Answer: B**

---

### 27. Capillaries are specialized to:
A) Pump blood  
B) Store blood  
C) Exchange gases, nutrients, and wastes  
D) Filter harmful substances  

**Answer: C**

---

### 28. Deoxygenated blood returns to the heart through:
A) Aorta  
B) Pulmonary artery  
C) Vena cava  
D) Pulmonary vein  

**Answer: C**

---

### 29. Oxygenated blood returns to the heart from lungs through:
A) Pulmonary artery  
B) Pulmonary vein  
C) Aorta  
D) Vena cava  

**Answer: B**

---

### 30. Blood oxygenation occurs in:
A) The heart  
B) The liver  
C) The lungs  
D) The aorta  

**Answer: C**

---

## Topic 18: Blood

### 31. Blood is composed of:
A) Cells only  
B) Plasma only  
C) Plasma and formed elements  
D) Water only  

**Answer: C**

---

### 32. Plasma constitutes approximately:
A) 20%  
B) 40%  
C) 55%  
D) 80%  

**Answer: C**

---

### 33. Red blood cells are also called:
A) Leukocytes  
B) Thrombocytes  
C) Erythrocytes  
D) Lymphocytes  

**Answer: C**

---

### 34. The main function of RBCs is to:
A) Fight infections  
B) Form clots  
C) Carry oxygen  
D) Digest nutrients  

**Answer: C**

---

### 35. White blood cells are responsible for:
A) Oxygen transport  
B) Clotting  
C) Defense against infection  
D) Digestion  

**Answer: C**

---

### 36. Platelets are:
A) Complete cells  
B) Cell fragments  
C) Bacteria  
D) Viruses  

**Answer: B**

---

### 37. The four major blood types are:
A) 1, 2, 3, 4  
B) X, Y, Z, W  
C) A, B, AB, O  
D) Red, blue, green, yellow  

**Answer: C**

---

### 38. Blood type is determined by:
A) Plasma proteins  
B) Antigens on RBC surface  
C) Hemoglobin  
D) Platelets  

**Answer: B**

---

### 39. Type O blood has:
A) A antigen  
B) B antigen  
C) Both antigens  
D) No antigens  

**Answer: D**

---

### 40. Type AB blood has:
A) Only A antigen  
B) Only B antigen
